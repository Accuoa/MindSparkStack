The Role-Context-Constraints Framework

The RCC Framework
R
Role
Who or what the AI should be. Sets tone, expertise, perspective.
C
Context
The situation, background, goal, and audience. The "why."
C
Constraints
Format, length, tone, scope limits. What to avoid.
You are [Role: e.g., a senior copywriter specializing in B2B SaaS].
I need you to [Task] for [Audience].
Background: [Relevant context, goal, situation].
Constraints: [Format / length / tone / what to avoid].

Context Checklist
  • Who is the audience? (role, knowledge level, goals)
  • What is the end goal? (what should happen after they read/use this?)
  • What's the situation or problem? (relevant background)
  • What have you already tried or know?
  • What's the desired format? (email, bullet list, code, table)
  • What's the tone? (formal, casual, direct, empathetic)
  • Any specific requirements or non-negotiables?
Constraint Types
Format Bullet points, numbered list, table, paragraph, code block, JSON, markdown headings
Length Under 200 words, one paragraph, 3 sentences max, full-length report — be explicit
Tone Professional, conversational, technical, encouraging, blunt, concise — state it clearly
Scope Only focus on X, don't include Y, assume the reader knows Z, exclude recommendations
Avoid No filler phrases, no hedging, no jargon, don't repeat what I said, no preamble
Quick-Reference Prompts
Full RCC in action
"You are a no-nonsense executive coach. I'm a first-time manager who just got promoted and needs to give feedback to an underperformer. Write a 3-sentence script I can use in a 1:1. Keep it direct, not cruel. No preamble."
Add constraints to any prompt
"[Your existing prompt]. Format: bullet points only. Tone: direct and practical. Length: under 150 words. Skip any caveats or disclaimers."
Test your role assignment
"Before we start — what expertise and perspective are you bringing to this task based on the role I gave you? Confirm your approach in one sentence."
Common Mistakes
  • Assigning a vague role like "expert" without specifying the domain or perspective.
  • Providing context without a goal — AI can't prioritize what matters most.
  • Skipping constraints entirely, then complaining the output is too long or off-tone.
  • Combining contradictory constraints (e.g., "be comprehensive but under 50 words").
