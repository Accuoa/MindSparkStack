Copy-Paste Ready Prompts by Task Type

Email
Write a professional email
"Write a concise, professional email to [recipient/role] asking for [request]. Context: [relevant background]. Tone: direct and respectful. No filler phrases. End with a clear call to action."
Respond to a difficult message
"Here's an email I received: [paste email]. Help me respond professionally. Goal: [what I want to achieve]. Tone: calm and firm. Under 100 words."
≡
Reports
Structure a report
"I need to write a [type] report for [audience] covering [topic]. Create a clear structure with section headers and 2-sentence descriptions of what each section should cover."
Executive summary from notes
"Here are my raw notes: [paste notes]. Write a 3-paragraph executive summary. Lead with the key finding, then context, then recommended action."
⊞
Data Analysis
Interpret data findings
"Here is my dataset summary: [paste data or description]. Identify the 3 most significant patterns or insights. Explain what each means in plain language and suggest one action for each."
Write a formula / query
"Write a [Excel/Google Sheets/SQL] formula/query that does the following: [describe what you need]. The data is structured as [columns/schema]."

Research
Research briefing
"Give me a balanced briefing on [topic]. Cover: (1) what it is, (2) the strongest arguments for, (3) the strongest arguments against, (4) where experts disagree. Flag areas where I should verify independently."
Competitive landscape
"Summarize the competitive landscape for [industry/product category]. Include: key players, their positioning, notable differentiators, and any recent shifts. Output as a structured table."
◎
Meetings
Meeting agenda
"Create a tight agenda for a [length] meeting about [topic] with [attendees/roles]. Goal of the meeting: [decision/outcome needed]. Format: time-boxed bullets."
Action items from notes
"Here are my meeting notes: [paste notes]. Extract all action items as a bulleted list. Format each as: Owner — Task — Deadline. If a deadline or owner is unclear, flag it."
Key Concepts
  • The best prompts are specific to the task — avoid one-size-fits-all templates.
  • Always specify the audience — "write for my CEO" changes everything vs. "write for a technical team."
  • Include the desired format explicitly: table, email, bullet list, numbered steps.
  • Give AI the raw material (notes, data, draft) and let it process — don't describe what you have.
  • When output isn't right, add a constraint rather than rewriting from scratch.
Common Mistakes
  • Describing the task abstractly instead of pasting the actual content to work with.
  • Forgetting to specify the audience — AI writes for a generic reader by default.
  • Using AI to generate content you have no way to verify (stats, citations, specific facts).
  • Not iterating — the first draft is not the deliverable.
