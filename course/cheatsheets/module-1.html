Module 1 — Cheat Sheet

Why Most People Use AI Wrong

The Input-Output Principle
Input Quality
Vague prompt → vague result. Specificity is the multiplier.
→
AI Processing
Probabilistic reasoning. It fills gaps with its best guess.
→
Output Quality
Directly proportional to the context you gave it.
  • AI doesn't know your situation — you have to tell it. Every missing detail is a guess.
  • Think of it as briefing a talented contractor who just started today. They're skilled, but context-blind.
  • The gap between average and expert AI output is almost always the prompt, not the model.
5 Core Concepts
1
AI as Collaborator, Not Oracle It reasons with you, not for you. Your judgment shapes the output.
2
Garbage In, Garbage Out Output quality ceiling = input quality. No exceptions.
3
Iteration Over Perfection First output is a draft. Refine with follow-up prompts.
4
Context Is Capital More relevant context = more relevant output. Front-load it.
5
You Stay in the Loop AI executes. You verify. Final judgment is always yours.
3 Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Treating AI like Google — asking one-line questions and expecting deep answers. AI needs context to give useful output.
  • Accepting the first response without iteration. Draft one is a starting point, not a final deliverable.
  • Over-trusting outputs without verification. AI hallucinates. Always sanity-check facts, numbers, and citations.
  • Using the same generic prompt for everything. Different tasks need different structures. One size never fits all.
Quick-Reference Prompts
Reframe AI as collaborator
"I'm going to share context about [situation]. Don't give me an answer yet — ask me any clarifying questions you need to give the most useful response possible."
Improve a bad first output
"This response isn't quite right. Specifically: [what's wrong]. Here's what I actually need: [clarification]. Try again with that in mind."
Force verification thinking
"List the assumptions you made in that response and flag any areas where you're uncertain or where I should verify independently."

  • Pick one task you already use AI for and rewrite your prompt from scratch using full context (who you are, what you need, why, format expected).
  • Practice the "iterate, don't accept" habit: after every AI response this week, ask at least one follow-up to improve it.
  • Try the "ask clarifying questions first" prompt above on a complex task and notice the difference in output quality.
  • Identify the #1 type of task where you've been getting mediocre AI output. That's your first prompt to fix in Module 2.
